Some work in brief —

IBM Presence Insights (Ongoing)

A product/platform which will enable people working "on the floor" (in retail, fulfillment centres, museums, airports, etc.) to get a deeper sense of the world around them. Comprised of a set of tools which enable people to weave together physical and digital experiences/systems in an intuitive and beautiful manner. View the product or read my case study.

—

IBM RapidApps

An idealistic product/platform built to “Democratize the process of application development”. An attempt at empowering (and educating) non-technical people to build applications to solve everyday life/business problems. RapidApps utilized “spatially-concrete” interface design metaphors so that abstract software development concepts were made easier to understand. Spoiler alert: The product ended up failing. View the beta product or read my postmortem.

—

Mobility and Catheter

With a small and independently-funded team, conducted research towards a holistic redesign of the “catheter management experience”, focusing on ease of use, cleanliness, reduction of UTI, accessibility, discreetness, and a user’s dignity. Led the design of the physical product and user experience. Read my case study.

Smart Design, OXO

Built prototypes, conducted research, produced visuals for various projects/clients, and co-led the development of various OXO consumer products, a few of which are in now in production. Gained considerable insight into the design of both soft and hard goods and the design of ergonomic tools. Check out (or buy!) any of the products (Baster, Pizza Cutter, Feather Duster, Salad Servers) I worked on or read about my experience here.
