Intro —

It was the summer of 2013, and I had found myself in the lobby of a glass building with 80-or-so strangers. A few months prior, I had decided to jump straight into a 'real-life job' right after university — a job that entailed the design and development of digital products.

Given that, before this summer, I had dedicated four years of my life to the pinpoint focus and study of physical product design, the entire prospect of jumping into software design was pretty terrifying.

I had no idea what I was getting myself into.

—

Initially —

Initially, IBM Design was for all intents and purposes the Wild West.

Without much direction, us first wave designers needed to chart out how Design Culture would be established in a company where designers were largely thought of as icon builders. It was explicitly mentioned by Phil Gilbert, our General Manager, that the odds were pretty stacked against us.

This was the environment we were dropped into.
